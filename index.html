Academic Projects

For me, completing projects is one of the most interactive & effective ways to implement the knowledge that I learn in classroom into real-world systems. Below you will find some of my completed projects that have provided me valuable knowledge & exposure to the challenges faced when applying engineering concepts in real-world.

Development of an Autonomous Hexapod Robot

Built a six legged robot with 3 degrees-of-freedom/leg using manufacturing processes like Laser Cutting and Fused Deposition Modeling. Implemented algorithms like Visual-SLAM (using the Extended Kalamn Filter) for localizing & mapping and navigation planner using the trajectory rollout approach & D* search algorithm to achieve autonomy in environments void of GPS.

Localization, Mapping, and Motion Planning for Wheeled Robots

Implemented Particle Filter fusing measurement data from a depth sensor & a beacon sensor for localizing the robot in an environment. Used a geometric approach to update the map constantly based on the readings from the depth sensor. Probabilistic Roadmaps & Dijkstra's Algorithm were used to help the robot navigate in the environment and travel to desired locations.

Vehicle Steering using Model Predictive Control

Designed and implemented a Model Predictive Controller (MPC) while taking into account the physical constraints for steering an automobile such that it changes lanes and avoids oncoming obstacles. Also formulated a mathematical model of the disturbances parallel to the vehicle due to strong winds which provided a better understanding of the performance of the controller in real-world scenarios.

Robot Writing

The goal of the project was to teach a robotic manipulator (Widowx 250s) with 6 degree-of-freedom to replicate the alphanumeric characters written by a human. Four approaches were implemented to achieve this: Dynamic Movement Primitives, Recurrent Neural Networks, Model Predictive Control, and simple waypoint tracking. We also performed comparative evaluation of each of these techniques for a given set of characters.

Color sorting robotic manipulator

A 3 degree of freedom robotic manipulator was designed and constructed using stainless steel bars as the links and DC servo motors as the actuators. A gripper was developed using gear mechanism to pick up objects and place them into specified locations based on their color. Arduino was used to control the motion and perform decision-making process based on the measurement values from the color sensor.

Smartphone controlled Peaucellier Mechanism

Constructed a physical model of the peaucellier mechanism using wooden linkages. Used a servo motor controlled by an Arduino Uno to drive the mechanism. A Bluetooth module was connected to the Arduino board which enabled us to control the motion of the mechanism through commands given by our smartphone.

Detection, Classification, Localization and tracking using multiple pinhole camera

A sensor model of the pinhole pan & tilt camera was developed in MATLAB for detection, localization and tracking of targets (both stationary and moving targets) as a point as well as a rigid body. The algorithm takes into account the physical constraints of the sensor and enables it to track efficiently the target in the workspace. A basic pretrained convolutional neural network is also used to classify the detected targets throughout the simulation.

Hydraulic Press System for demonstration of laws of Fluid Mechanics

Constructed a model of the hydraulic press system using PVC pipes. Attached pressure gauges to demonstrate Pascal's Law and the Hydrostatic Paradox of fluid mechanics.
